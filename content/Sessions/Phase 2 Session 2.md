CVD: Takes each candles delta and then adds them all up to see who is winning throughout the day You read CVD vertically against each other

Divergence is when PRICE does not line up with what the CVD is saying. Candles are moving in opposites to each other.


- **The "Red Delta/Green Candle":** Massive aggressive selling happened, but the price went up. (This is **Absorption**).
    
- **The "Green Delta/Red Candle":** Massive aggressive buying happened, but the price went down. (This is **Exhaustion** or a Big Seller holding the line).

Whale and bathtub analogy. Low liquidity markets are dangerous because of low conformations.
#### **High Liquidity (The Swimming Pool)**

- **Market:** S&P 500 (ES) or Apple Stock.
    
- **The Scenario:** A "Whale" (a massive bank) wants to sell $100$ Million dollars worth of stock.
    
- **The Result:** Because the pool is so big and deep (lots of Limit Orders on the DOM), the whale jumps in and the water level only rises an inch. The price moves slowly and smoothly. Your Footprint gives you clear, reliable "confirmations."
    

#### **Low Liquidity (The Bathtub)**

- **Market:** Small-cap stocks, "Penny" stocks, or new Altcoins.
    
- **The Scenario:** That same "Whale" wants to sell $100$ Million dollars.
    
- **The Result:** The bathtub is tiny. When the whale jumps in, **all the water flies out of the tub.** * The price **gaps** (skips levels).
    
    - There is massive **Slippage** (your stop loss gets hit way further down than you wanted).
        
    - The Footprint looks like "Swiss Cheese" (lots of zeros), so you can't actually see a trend forming. It’s just chaos.


$$\text{Spread} = \text{Best Ask (Lowest Seller)} - \text{Best Bid (Highest Buyer)}$$ larger spreads = poor liquidity
smaller spreads = tighter liquidity 


high spread = high volatility
