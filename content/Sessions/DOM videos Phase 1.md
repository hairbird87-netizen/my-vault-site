### Key points (notes from the video)

- Ask orders = publicly shown sell limit orders (where sellers are willing to sell).  
- Bid orders = publicly shown buy limit orders (where buyers are willing to buy).  
- Block fill level indicates relative queue size at each price (more filled = larger resting interest).

### One-line summary
The DOM (order book) displays the ladder of resting buy (bids) and sell (asks) orders around the current trading price; reading it shows where liquidity is stacked and whether the market is skewed toward buyers or sellers.

### Key points
- The DOM shows bids on the left and asks on the right; the current price is the equilibrium.  
- Ask orders = visible sell-limit orders; bid orders = visible buy-limit orders.  
- Block fill indicates queue size at each price; empty cells mean no resting interest.  
- Delta = ask executed volume − bid executed volume (shows aggressor side imbalance).  
- Limit orders are passive: they sit in the queue until price hits them (then they may execute).  
- Price moves when aggressive orders meet resting orders (market orders take liquidity).  
- If more buys meet sells → buyer skew (price tends up); if more sells meet buys → seller skew (price tends down).  
- Footprint charts show per-price delta and executed volume; scalpers prefer simple, fast-read tools.

### Practical implications / micro-actions
- Watch for large clustered bids/asks (liquidity pools) or sudden disappearances (liquidity migration).  
- For theory videos: focus on being able to **explain** each DOM column and control, not yet trading it.
